Frequently Asked Questions

How do I iniate the adoption process?

A complete application is required to start the adoption process and submission of an application does not guarantee the adoption of a dog.

How old do I have to be to adopt?

You must be 23yrs of age to adopt.

Is there an application fee or fee for applying?

No, there is no fee to apply.

Are all animals vaccinated prior to adoption?

Yes, all animals are vaccinated for rabies, parvo/distemper and Bordetella prior to adoption.

Can I just adopt an animal without my family's input?

No, all household members should be in agreement about the decision to adopt and be willing to play an active role in the adoption process.

What vetinary information do you require?

A verifiable history of vetting is required for all current and previous pets. This includes a verifiable history of appropriate wellness visits, immunizations, heart worm testing and a verifiable history of administering heart worm prevention to all dogs.

Will my dog need to adjust?

It will take time for your new dog to adjust to its new home, especially if you have other pets.(Usually 2-4 weeks, and sometimes longer.) They may require extra exercise or fewer visitors to your home at first. This must be an adjustment you're willing to make to your daily routine.

What can I expect in the adoption process

We welcome all adopters! Our goal is to make the best match we can between our adopters and our shelter pets. We do this by providing all information that we know about each animal and letting the adopter decide if the pet is a match for them. We want to be a lifelong resource for you and your pets!

How long does the adoption process take?

The length of time that an adoption takes varies widely by type of pet, the number of meet-and-greets required, whether the pet is in the shelter or in foster care, and the availability of open appointments. Most shelter pets receive multiple applications, and we recommend that you explore other available shelter pets if you have not heard back within 72 hours.

I have other pets. Is that okay?

Yes! We want to make sure that the animal you're interested in adopting would be compatible with any current pets in the household. Dogs, rabbits, and cats may require a same species meet up.

What is included in the adoption fee?

  • Spay/neuter surgery for dogs, cats and rabbits.
  • Recommended vaccinations, dewormer and flea control.
  • Microchip and registration for dogs, cats, rabbits and ferrets.
  • Certificate for free initial veterinary health exam. (Restrictions apply, see certificate.)
